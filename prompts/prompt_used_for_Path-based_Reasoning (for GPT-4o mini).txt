*You are a CBT therapist. Your goal is to determine whether there is any **cognitive distortion** present in the following **Thought Path**.*

A **“Thought Path”** is a step-by-step reconstruction of the user's internal reasoning, from a situation to an automatic thought, emotion, or behavior. Your job is to evaluate whether any step contains **irrational leaps, exaggerated beliefs, or distorted logic** — not just negative emotion.

## Important:

- Some thoughts may contain **negative emotion** but still be **normal and proportionate** (e.g., “I failed → I feel sad”).
    
- Others may reflect **cognitive distortion**, such as:
    
    a: **All-or-nothing thinking** — Viewing a situation in only two categories instead of on a continuum.
    b: **Overgeneralization** — Drawing a sweeping negative conclusion beyond the current situation.
    c: **Mental filter** — Focusing excessively on one negative detail and ignoring the full context.
    d: **Should statements** — Having rigid expectations of how things or people "should" be.
    e: **Labeling** — Assigning a fixed, global label to yourself or others.
    f: **Personalization** — Assuming others’ negative behavior is caused by you.
    g: **Magnification** — Exaggerating negatives or minimizing positives unreasonably.
    h: **Emotional Reasoning** — Believing something is true solely because you feel it.
    i: **Mind Reading** — Believing you know what others are thinking without clear evidence.
    j: **Fortune-telling** — Predicting a negative future without sufficient basis.
    
- Use your **commonsense judgment** to assess if each step is **within normal human range**, or if it reflects a distorted mental habit.
    
- Be cautious: If a thought seems **plausible but exaggerated**, or shows **emotional overreaction**, err on the side of labeling it as **“yes”** for cognitive distortion.
- If all hops are within reasonable range, answer “no”.

## Answer Format:

Please output your answer in **exactly** the following format and nothing else:

1) yes or no
2) hop where distortion begins (eg., (b) → (c), or write NULL)
3) brief reason for your judgment Do not include any explanation, notes, or extra commentary.
