# LLMs-Are-Making-You-Sick-Uncovering-the-Overpathologizing-Bias-in-Cognitive-Distortion-Detection

As mental health problems become increasingly prevalent worldwide, recent studies have applied Large Language Models (LLMs) to detect cognitive distortions—irrational thinking patterns that serve as early indicators of mental disorders—from user-generated text. However, LLMs often exhibit overpathologizing bias, misclassifying normal emotions or behaviors as pathological. Such misclassification can induce unnecessary self-stigma in non-clinical users, leading them to view themselves as mentally ill. Analyzing several state-of-the-art LLMs, we identify two main causes of overpathologizing bias: (1) coarse-grained interpretation that overlooks psychological context, and (2) overinterpretation during one-directional cause-to-result reasoning.
To address these issues, we propose **REFORM** (REverse reasoning and Fine-grained thought path for OveRpathologizing Mitigation), a dual-complementary framework combining fine-grained thought path for context-aware reasoning and reverse reasoning for post detection of overpathologizing.
